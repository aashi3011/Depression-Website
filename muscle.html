Let's Begin...

Hip Flexors:

The hip flexors are several muscles that bring your legs and trunk together in a flexion movement. They allow you to move your leg or knee up towards your torso, as well as to bend your torso forward at the hip. You can strain or tear your hip flexor muscles through sudden movements or falls.

Calf Stretch:

Calf raises are a method of exercising the gastrocnemius, tibialis posterior and soleus muscles of the lower leg. The movement performed is plantar flexion. This stretch is used by martial arts students in order to loosen their calf muscles (because a torn calf muscle is common martial arts injury).

Quadriceps Stretch:

Standing Quad Stretch. While standing, hold onto a countertop or chair back to assist in balance. Bend your knee back by grasping your ankle with one hand. Assist in bending your knee back as far as possible. Maintain position for 30 seconds. Return to standing position. Repeat exercise 3 to 5 times with each leg.
